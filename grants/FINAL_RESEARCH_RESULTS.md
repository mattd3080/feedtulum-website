## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. The "Invitation-Only" Barrier is MASSIVE
Over half (52%) of major foundations do not accept unsolicited proposals. They proactively identify organizations to invite. This creates an insurmountable barrier for newer/smaller organizations without established relationships.

### 2. US-Based Working Internationally is RARE
Most US foundations fund either:
- US domestic programs only
- International programs through large established partners
- Very few fund small US-based orgs doing direct international work

### 3. The Kellogg Opportunity Could Be Huge
If W.K. Kellogg accepts US 501(c)(3)s for Mexico work, Feed Tulum has a PERFECT fit:
- Tulum is in Quintana Roo (Yucatan Peninsula = their priority region!)
- Programs align: food systems, children, education
- Rolling deadline = flexible timing

### 4. Catholic Foundations Offer Potential
Raskob and Koch explicitly include Mexico, but both have complications:
- Raskob: Unclear if US orgs qualify or only Mexican Catholic entities
- Koch: Requires US Catholic fiscal agent

### 5. Corporate Foundations are Fragmented
Most operate separate foundations in each country (Walmart Mexico, etc.), making it difficult for US-based orgs to access.
