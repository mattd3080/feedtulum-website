# PART 2: ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## 2.1 Absolute Requirements (MUST Have All)

A grant ONLY qualifies if it meets ALL of these:

| # | Requirement | Verification Method |
|---|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Deadline is in the future** | Check grant website for current cycle dates. Today's date: [CHECK CURRENT DATE]. Deadline must be AFTER today. |
| 2 | **Accepts Mexico/Latin America projects** | Read geographic eligibility section. Must explicitly include Mexico, Latin America, Central America, or "international." |
| 3 | **Accepts US 501(c)(3) organizations** | Check organizational eligibility. Many international grants require registration IN the country of operation - these do NOT qualify. |
| 4 | **Open application process** | Look for public "Apply" button or form. If says "by invitation only," "nomination required," or "invitation from program officer" - does NOT qualify. |
| 5 | **Mission alignment exists** | At least ONE Feed Tulum program must match their funding priorities. |
| 6 | **No disqualifying minimums** | Check minimum grant size and minimum organizational budget requirements. |

## 2.2 Automatic Disqualifiers (Instant Reject)

If you see ANY of these, mark as DISQUALIFIED immediately:

**Application Type Disqualifiers:**
- "By invitation only"
- "Invitation-only application process"
- "Nominations accepted from [specific nominators]"
- "Organizations must be invited to apply"
- "Contact program officer before applying" (unless open inquiry process)
- "We do not accept unsolicited proposals"

**Geographic Disqualifiers:**
- "US operations only"
- "Domestic programs only"
- "Must operate in [US state/region]"
- "Must be registered in the country of operation" (Feed Tulum is US-registered, operates in Mexico)
- Specific country list that excludes Mexico

**Organizational Disqualifiers:**
- "Government agencies only"
- "Universities and research institutions only"
- "Schools/school districts only"
- "Hospitals and healthcare systems only"
- "Must have 501(c)(3) status for [X] years" (if Feed Tulum doesn't meet threshold)
- "Minimum annual budget of $[X]" (if exceeds Feed Tulum's budget)

**Timing Disqualifiers:**
- Deadline has already passed
- Grant cycle is closed
- "Applications for [year] are now closed"
- RFP expired

**Scope Disqualifiers:**
- "Advocacy and policy work only"
- "Research grants only"
- "Capital campaigns only" (unless Feed Tulum has capital needs)
- "Endowment grants only"

## 2.3 Positive Eligibility Signals (Good Signs)

Look for these indicators that a grant likely qualifies:

- "Open to international organizations"
- "US-based nonprofits working internationally"
- "Latin America" or "Mexico" in priority regions
- "Food security" or "hunger" in focus areas
- "Children" or "youth" in focus areas
- "Community-based organizations"
- "Grassroots organizations"
- "Small/emerging nonprofits welcome"
- Rolling deadline or "applications accepted year-round"
- Grant size range $5,000 - $100,000 (appropriate for small org)
- "Letter of inquiry" process (usually more accessible)

# PART 4: RESEARCH EXECUTION PROCESS

## 4.1 Level 1: Quick Scan (30 seconds per grant)

**Actions:**
1. Read grant name and funder name
2. Scan for geographic eligibility (Mexico/Latin America mentioned?)
3. Look for "Apply" button vs "By invitation"
4. Note deadline if visible
5. Check grant amount range

**Decision Tree:**
```
Is Mexico/Latin America/International mentioned?
  NO → SKIP (don't research further)
  YES → Continue

Does it say "invitation only" or "nomination required"?
  YES → DISQUALIFIED
  NO → Continue

Is there a visible deadline that has passed?
  YES → DISQUALIFIED
  NO → Continue to Level 2
```

**Output:** List of grants worth deeper investigation

## 4.2 Level 2: Eligibility Verification (2-3 minutes per grant)

**Actions:**
1. Navigate to full eligibility criteria page
2. Read complete geographic eligibility section
3. Read complete organizational eligibility section
4. Verify current deadline
5. Check grant amount range
6. Run through 6-point eligibility checklist

**6-Point Checklist:**
| # | Check | Result |
|---|-------|--------|
| 1 | Deadline future? | YES/NO |
| 2 | Mexico/Latin America eligible? | YES/NO |
| 3 | US 501(c)(3) accepted? | YES/NO |
| 4 | Open application? | YES/NO |
| 5 | Mission alignment? | YES/NO |
| 6 | No disqualifying minimums? | YES/NO |

**Decision:**
- All 6 YES → QUALIFIED (proceed to Level 3)
- Any NO → DISQUALIFIED (document reason)

**Output:** Qualified/Disqualified status with reason

## 4.3 Level 3: Requirements Deep Dive (10-15 minutes per grant)

## 4.4 Level 4: Priority Scoring (5 minutes per grant)

**Score each qualified grant:**

| Factor | Weight | Score 1-5 | How to Score |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Deadline Urgency | 25% | 1-5 | <30 days=5, 30-60=4, 60-90=3, 90-180=2, 180+=1, Rolling=3 |
| Mission Fit | 30% | 1-5 | Perfect match=5, Strong=4, Good=3, Moderate=2, Weak=1 |
| Grant Amount | 20% | 1-5 | $50K+=5, $25-50K=4, $10-25K=3, $5-10K=2, <$5K=1 |
| Application Complexity | 15% | 1-5 | Very simple=5, Simple=4, Moderate=3, Complex=2, Very complex=1 |
| Success Likelihood | 10% | 1-5 | Very likely=5, Likely=4, Possible=3, Difficult=2, Long shot=1 |

**Calculate:**
```
Priority Score = (Urgency × 0.25) + (Fit × 0.30) + (Amount × 0.20) + (Simplicity × 0.15) + (Likelihood × 0.10)
```

**Ranking:**
- 4.0+ = HIGH PRIORITY (apply first)
- 3.0-3.9 = MEDIUM PRIORITY
- 2.0-2.9 = LOW PRIORITY
- Below 2.0 = RECONSIDER

# APPENDIX B: Common Grant Terms Glossary

**LOI (Letter of Inquiry):** Short preliminary proposal (1-3 pages) submitted before full application. Often used to screen applicants.

**RFP (Request for Proposals):** Formal solicitation inviting organizations to submit proposals.

**General Operating Support:** Funding for overall organizational operations (most flexible).

**Program/Project Support:** Funding for specific programs or projects.

**Capacity Building:** Funding to strengthen organizational effectiveness.

**Rolling Deadline:** Applications accepted continuously (no fixed deadline).

**Invitation Only:** Funder selects organizations to invite; cannot apply unsolicited.

**Fiscal Sponsor:** Organization that provides 501(c)(3) status to unincorporated projects.

# APPENDIX D: Red Flags to Watch For

**Application Red Flags:**
- No clear eligibility criteria published
- Application requires fee to submit
- Requests sensitive info before relationship established
- No clear contact information
- Website looks unprofessional or outdated
- Can't find evidence of past grantees

**Scam Indicators:**
- "You've been selected to receive a grant" (unsolicited)
- Requires upfront payment
- Asks for bank account details early
- Poor grammar/spelling in official communications
- Uses generic email domain (gmail, yahoo) for "foundation"
